## Workshops

### Exploring DH 

Digital Humanities (DH) is an interdisciplinary academic field that applies the computational tools and skills of data science and related fields to humanities and arts disciplines. It also applies critical thinking from the arts and humanities to the digital world that we live in today. DH is largely concerned with pedagogy and scholarship and inspires innovation in teaching, learning, and research. This workshop will cover the foundations of digital humanities and some common methods and tools.  

### Transcription for Digital Projects 

Transcription is a fundamental skill in digital humanities work and projects. Reliable transcriptions enhance readability, searchability, and accessibility in texts presented online. This workshop will introduce transcription methods and tools. Participants will learn the introductory concepts and get some hands-on practice. No previous experience is necessary. 

### Getting Started with Text Analysis  

Text analysis is the process of exploring written texts to uncover new insights by applying computational techniques that detect patterns, trends, and connections across large sets of documents. Text analysis can reveal patterns not easily discoverable by a single reader across a large collection of documents. Common approaches to text analysis include term and token counting, sentiment analysis, topic modeling, geographic analysis, named entity recognition, and ngrams. 

This workshop will cover text analysis tools and methods. There will be time for hands-on practice with the tools mentioned. 

### Crafting and Humanities Data 

Humanities data can take many forms, including images, audio files, text and manuscripts, maps, and more. It is often qualitative, but it can be quantitative as well (i.e. publishing records, ship inventories). #DHMAKES takes humanities data and brings it into crafting and making practices. This workshop will cover creative uses and inclusions of humanities data and discuss the intersection of research and maker movements.  

### TEI and the Digital Humanities 

The Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) is a non-profit organization that develops and maintains guidelines for the representation of texts in digital form. These guidelines, which provide the infrastructure for developing machine-actionable cultural heritage texts, are widely used by libraries, museums, publishers, and individual scholars for online research, teaching, and preservation. In addition to the Guidelines themselves, the Consortium provides a variety of resources and training events for learning TEI, information on projects using the TEI, and software developed for or adapted to the TEI. This workshop will introduce the Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) and its uses in the digital humanities. 

### Text Mining and Copyright 

Text and data mining (TDM) is an area of research that looks for patterns of meaning in large datasets or groups of texts. TDM introduces a wide range of research opportunities from mining articles and newspapers to creating better systematic reviews. However, along with these opportunities comes some legal constraints researchers should be aware of before beginning their projects. This workshop will discuss copyright and license restrictions of library subscription databases for TDM. We will introduce attendees to ways of navigating these legal considerations as well as highlight resources that qualify as fair-use options and exemptions to copyright.
